The fundamental theorem of arithmetic.

因为本课程采用英文教学以及英文考试, 故笔记采用全英文形式.

Introduction

The principle of induction

If $Q$ is a set of integers such that:

$$ \begin{aligned} &(a). 1 \in Q \\ &(b). n \in Q \ implies \ n + 1 \in Q, \\ &then \\ &(c) all \ integers \geq 1 \ belong \ to \ Q \end{aligned} $$

In statement (a), the integer 1 can be replaced by any integer $k$, provided that the inequality $\geq 1$ is replaced by $\geq k$ in (c). Also, (b) can be replaced by the statement $1, 2, 3, \cdots , n \in Q $ implied $(n + 1) \in Q$ .

The well-ordering principle

If A is a nonempty set of positive integers, then A contains a smallest member.

This principle also has equivalent formulations. For example, “positive integers” can be replaced by “$integers \geq k $ for some $k$ .”

Divisibility

Notation

In this chapter, small latin letters a, b, c, d, n, etc. , denote integers; they can be positive, negative, or zero.

Definition of divisibility

d divides n if and only if n = cd for some c , and we write d | n . We also say that n is a multiple of d , that d is a divisor of n , or that d is a factor of n . If d does not divide n we write d$\not|$ n .

Properties of Divisibility

  • Reflexive property: n | n .
  • Transitive property: d | n and n | m implies d | m .
  • Linearity property: d | n and d | m implies d | (an + bm) .
  • Multiplication property: d | n implies ad | an .
  • Cancellation law: ad | an and a $\not ={0}$ implies d | n .
  • 1 divides every integer: 1|n .
  • Every integer divides zero: n | 0.
  • Zero only divides zero: 0 | n implies n = 0.
  • Comparison property: d | n and n $\not ={0}$ implies |d| $\leq$ |n|.
  • d | n and n | d implies |d| = |n|.
  • d | n and d $\not ={0}$ implies (n/d)|n.

Greatest common divisor

Common divisor

Given any two integers a and b , there is a common divisor d of a and b of the form:

$$ d = ax + by $$

where x and y are integers. Moreover, every common divisor of a and b divides this d .

Greatest common divisor

Given integers a and b , there is one and only one number d with the following properties:

$$ \begin{aligned} &(a) d \geq 0 \\ &(b) d|a \ and \ d|b \\ &(c) e|a \ and \ e|b \ implies \ e|d \end{aligned} $$

The number of d is called the greatest common divisor (gcd) of a and b an is denoted by (a, b) or by aDb. if (a, b) = 1 then a and b are said to be relatively prime.

Properties of gcd

  • (a, b) = (b, a).
  • (a, (b, c)) = ((a, b), c).
  • (ac, bc) = |c|(a, b).
  • (a, 1) = (1, a) = 1.

Euclid’s lemma

if a|bc and if (a, b) = 1, then a|c.

Prime numbers

Definition of prime numbers

An integer n is called prime if n > 1 and if the only positive divisors of n are 1 and n . If n > 1 and if n is not prime, then n is called composite.
Prime numbers are usually denoted by $p, p’, p_i, q, q’, q_i$
Every integer n > 1 is either a prime number or a product of prime numbers.
There are infinitely many prime numbers.

Theorems of prime numbers

  • If a prime $p$ does not divide $a$ , then $(p, a) = 1$ .
  • If a prime $p$ divides a product $a_1 \cdots a_n$ , then $p$ divides at least one of the factors.

The fundamental theorem of arithmetic

  • Fundamental theorem of arithmetic: every integer $n > 1$ can be represented as a product of prime factors in only one way, apart from the order of the factors.
  • If $n = \prod_{i = 1}^{r}p_i^{a_i}$ , the set of positive divisors of $n$ is the set of numbers of the form $\prod_{i = 1}^{r}p_i^{c_i}$ , where $0 \leq c_i \leq a_i$ for $i = 1, 2, \cdots , r$ .
  • If two positive integers $a$ and $b$ have the factorizations $a = \prod_{i = 1}^{a_i\infty}p_i^{a_i} , \ b = \prod_{i = 1}^{a_i\infty}p_i^{b_i}$ , then their gcd has the factorization $(a, b) = \prod_{i = 1}^{a_i\infty}p_i^{c_i}$ where each $c_i = min{a_i, b_i}$ .

The series of reciprocals

The infinite series $\sum_{n = 1}^{\infty}{1/p_n}$ diverges.

The Euclidean algorithm

The division algorithm

Given integers $a$ and $b$ with $b > 0$ , there exists a unique pair of integers $q$ and $r$ such that

$$ a = bq + r, with \ 0 \leq r < b. $$

Moreover, $r = 0$ if and only if $b|a$ .
Note. We say that $q$ is the quotient and $r$ the remainder obtained when $b$ is divided into $a$ .

The Euclidean algorithm

Give positive integers $a$ and $b$ , where $b \not | \ a$ . Let $r_0 = a, r_1 = b$ , and apply the division algorithm repeatedly to obtain a set of remainders $r_2, r_3, \cdots , r_n, r_{n + 1}$ defined successively by the relations:

$$ r_0 = r_1q_1 + r_2, \ \ \ 0 < r_2 < r_1, \\ r_1 = r_2q_2 + r_3, \ \ \ 0 < r_3 < r_2, \\ \vdots \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \\ r_{n - 2} = r_{n - 1}q_{n - 1} + r_n, \ \ \ 0 < r_n < r_{n - 1}, \\ r_{n - 1} = r_nq_n + r_{n + 1}, \ \ \ r_{n + 1} = 0. $$

Then $r_n$ , the last nonzero remainder in this process, is $(a, b)$ , the gcd of $a$ and $b$ .

The greatest common divisor of more than two numbers

The gcd of n integers $a_1, \cdots , a_n$ is defined inductively by the relation

$$ (a_1, \cdots , a_n) = (a_1, (a_2, \cdots , a_n)) $$

This number is independent of the order in which the $a_i$ appear.

If $d = (a_1 , \cdots , a_n)$ it is easy to verify that $d$ divides each of the $a_i$ and that every common divisor divides $d$ . Moreover, $d$ is a linear combination of the $a_i$ . That is, there exist integers $x_1, \cdots , x_n$ such that

$$ (a_1, \cdots , a_n) = a_1x_1 + \cdots + a_nx_n $$

If $d = 1$ the numbers are said to be relatively prime .

If $(a_i, a_j) = 1$ whenever $i \not ={j}$ the numbers $a_1, \cdots , a_n$ are said to be relatively prime in pairs . If $a_1, \cdots , a_n$ are relatively prime in pairs then $(a_1, \cdots , a_n) = 1$ . However, the converse is not necessarily true.

m-adic representations

Let $m$ be an integer with $m \geq 2$ . Every positive integer $n$ can be represented uniquely in the form $n = a_0 + a_1m + a_2m^2 + \cdots + a_km^k$ , where $m^k \leq n < m^{k+1}$ and $0 \leq a_i \leq m - 1, i = 0, 1, \cdots , k, a_k \not ={0}$ .

10-adic to m-adic

For example, 10-adic-number $a = 123456$ means:

$$ a = 1\times10^5 + 2\times10^4 + 310^3 + 410^2 + 510^1 + 610^0 $$

Also can be written as:

$$ a = 10(1\times10^4 + 2\times10^3 + 3\times10^3 + 4\times10^2 + 5\times10^1) + 6\times10^0 $$

If we want to transform it into 3-adic representation, we can:

$$ a = 3 \times 41152 + 0 \\ 41152 = 3 \times 13717 + 1 \\ 13717 = 3 \times 4572 + 1 \\ 4572 = 3 \times 1524 + 0 \\ 1524 = 3 \times 508 + 0 \\ 508 = 3 \times 169 + 1 \\ 169 = 3 \times 56 + 1 \\ 56 = 3 \times 18 + 2 \\ 18 = 3 \times 6 + 0 \\ 6 = 3 \times 2 + 0 \\ 2 = 3 \times 0 + 2 $$

So we can write 123456 as:

$$ \begin{aligned}123456 &= 3 \times (3 \times (3 \times (3 \\ &\ \ \ \ \times (3 \times (3 \times (3 \times (3 \\ &\ \ \ \ \times (3 \times (3 \times (3 \times (3 \\ &\ \ \ \ \times 0) + 2) + 0) + 0) \\ &\ \ \ \ + 2) + 1) + 1) + 0) \\ &\ \ \ \ + 0) + 1) + 1) + 0 \\ &= 2 \times 3^{10} + 0 \times 3^9 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 0 \times 3^8 + 2 \times 3^7 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 1 \times 3^6 + 1 \times 3^5 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 0 \times 3^4 + 0 \times 3^3 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 1 \times 3^2 + 1 \times 3^1 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 0 \times 3^0 \end{aligned}$$

As the result, the 3-adic representation of 123456 is 20021100110.

m-adic to 10-adic

3-adic-number $a = 20021100110$ can be transform into 10-adic representation as the following steps:

$$ \begin{aligned}a &= 0 \times 3^0 + 1 \times 3^1 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 1 \times 3^2 + 0 \times 3^3 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 0 \times 3^4 + 1 \times 3^5 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 1 \times 3^6 + 2 \times 3^7 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 0 \times 3^8 + 0 \times 3^9 \\ &\ \ \ \ + 2 \times 3^{10} \\ &= 123456\end{aligned} $$
